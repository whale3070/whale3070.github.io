雅思写作课程-第十八课

第一篇

原文：
This chart gives us information about the percentage of the population which lives in cities in 4 Asian countries Philippines, Malaysia, Thailand and Indonesia. The time range from 1970 to 2020, with predictions for 2030 and 2040.

Overall, the population which lives in cities in 4 Asian countries was fluctuated and dramatic increased.

In 1970, the number of Philippines’ population living in the cities was the most, and Indonesia was the lowest. It should be noted that among 4 countries, the number of population living in the cities was lower than 35%.
In 1980, the number of Malaysia’ population living in the cities was highest about 40%.
In 1990, It was the second time that Philippines’ population living in the cities was the most, and slightly higher than Malaysia.

But in the next 30 years, the number was totally different among two counties (Malaysia and Philippines ).
The number of Malaysia’ population living in the cities dramatic rose about 80%.
The number of Philippines’ population living in the cities always lower than 50% and decreased after 1990 in 20 years. It was slightly increased from 2010.

✅ 修改后的正确版本

This chart provides information about the percentage of the population living in cities in four Asian countries — the Philippines, Malaysia, Thailand, and Indonesia — from 1970 to 2020, with projections for 2030 and 2040.

Overall, the proportion of urban populations in these four countries increased significantly over the period, although the growth rates varied.

In 1970, the Philippines had the highest proportion of its population living in cities, while Indonesia had the lowest. It should be noted that in all four countries, less than 35% of people lived in urban areas.

By 1980, Malaysia’s urban population had become the highest, reaching around 40%.
In 1990, the Philippines once again recorded the highest figure, which was slightly higher than Malaysia’s.

However, over the next 30 years, the trends in Malaysia and the Philippines diverged.
The percentage of Malaysia’s population living in cities rose dramatically to around 80%, while the figure for the Philippines remained below 50% and declined slightly after 1990 before rising again after 2010.
